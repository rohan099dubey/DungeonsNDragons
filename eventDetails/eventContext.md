🐲 Event Overview: "The Dragon's Awakening"
Theme: Anime-style Adventurer's Guild (e.g., Solo Leveling, DanMachi).

Team Size: 3 Players per team.

Starting State: All players start as base-level "Humans" with a shared pool of base stats.

Ultimate Goal: Defeat the final Dragon.

Win Condition: Acquire at least 3 Pieces of Equipment from Dungeons, gain sufficient stats, and survive the Dragon encounter.

⚙️ The Core Mechanics
1. The Stat System (Shared Team Pool)
Instead of individual stats, the team operates as one unit with a shared ledger on their Adventurer's License (Dashboard).

Power (Attack)

HP (Health Points)

Mana (Magic)

Agility (Speed/Dexterity)

Brain Power (Smartness/Puzzle Solving)

2. The 5 Dungeons & Equipment
To fight the Dragon, teams must defeat Dungeon Masters to claim equipment. Each dungeon requires specific stats to enter/beat.

The Sword Dungeon: Unlocks the Sword (Grants "Swordsman" class to one member).

The Shield Dungeon: Unlocks the Shield (Grants "Tank/Defender" class to one member).

The Magic Wand Dungeon: Unlocks the Wand (Grants "Mage" class to one member).

The Spear Dungeon: Unlocks the Spear (Grants "Lancer" class to one member).

The Amulet Dungeon: Unlocks the Amulet (Grants "Support/Seer" class to one member).

🗺️ The Complete Event Workflow (Step-by-Step)
Phase 1: Guild Registration (Pre-Game)
Form a Party: Players form teams of 3 and register on the Guild Website.

Receive License: They log into their digital "Adventurer's License" which displays their starting base stats, empty equipment slots, and "Human" status.

The Briefing: The Guild Master (Event Host) explains the rules, points them to the digital Quest Board, and opens the event.

Phase 2: The Grind (Side Quests & Betting)
This is the main activity loop where players spend most of their time.

Choose a Quest: Teams look at the digital Quest Board to find physical mini-games, puzzles, or tasks hosted around your club venue.

Place the Bet: Every quest requires a stake. For example, a physical obstacle course might require betting 10 HP. A riddle room might require betting 10 Brain Power.

Execute the Quest: The team attempts the challenge.

Admin Resolution: * If they win: The Admin logs into the Ledger, clicks "Quest Success," and the team is rewarded (e.g., +30 Agility).

If they lose: The Admin clicks "Quest Failed," and the team loses their bet (-10 HP).

Track the Leaderboard: Teams check the Tavern Wall (live feed) to see who is gaining stats the fastest.

Phase 3: Dungeon Raiding (Boss Fights)
Once a team has farmed enough stats, they can challenge a Dungeon.

Check Requirements: A team sees that the Sword Dungeon requires 100 Power and 50 Agility to challenge.

Enter the Dungeon: If they have the stats, they approach the Dungeon Master (a specific organizer running a harder, premium challenge).

The Boss Fight: They play the Dungeon Master's challenge.

Loot & Class Assignment: If they win, the Admin updates their dashboard. The team receives the Sword icon in their inventory, and they must permanently assign the "Swordsman" role to one of their three members.

Global Announcement: The system automatically posts to the Tavern Wall: "Team Hero has cleared the Sword Dungeon!"

Phase 4: The Final Boss (The Dragon)
The Threshold: A team must successfully clear at least 3 of the 5 Dungeons (meaning they have 3 pieces of equipment and all 3 members now have official Classes/Roles).

The Dragon's Lair Opens: Once a team hits this threshold, they are permitted to enter the final stage to face the Dragon.

The Final Challenge: This should be the grand finale event of the club—a massive combined challenge (trivia, physical, puzzle) where they utilize their specific classes (Mage, Swordsman, etc.) to win the game.
